# Aeronautical data-source matrix

Status: Phase 0 research snapshot, 14 July 2026
Scope: candidate sources and fallbacks; inclusion is not approval to ingest or redistribute

## 1. Decision rules

- **Official** describes authority, not licence. Public web access does not prove commercial reuse, bulk access, modification, caching, or redistribution rights.
- **Production candidate** means technically plausible after the listed contractual, legal, quality, and SLA gates; it does not mean approved for primary navigation.
- The platform must retain source, cycle/edition, effective time, retrieval time, checksum, field-level provenance, licence snapshot, and known limitations.
- Never scrape or bundle an AIP, chart, NOTAM, weather product, proprietary code, or database when permission is absent or ambiguous.
- Fallback means a safer degraded product state. Community data is not an automatic operational substitute for missing authoritative data.

Suitability labels:

- **Yes-candidate**: rights and access appear compatible enough for an engineering evaluation; legal review still required.
- **Contract**: potentially suitable only under a signed data/API/redistribution agreement.
- **No-operational**: discovery, education, simulation, cross-checking, or user-link only.
- **Blocked**: critical rights/access/quality facts are unknown.

## 3. Coverage conclusions

### Best-supported initial jurisdiction

The United States has the strongest combination of structured official static data, official weather APIs, public-domain obstacle data, open terrain, current product schedules, and published EFB guidance. It is the recommended first engineering jurisdiction. This recommendation is conditional on product-level FAA rights review; free downloads alone are not treated as redistribution permission.

### Europe and UK

EUROCONTROL MyEAD is a credible professional system-to-system source but explicitly requires a Data User Agreement and can involve royalties. The UK must be treated separately, with NATS/CAA terms confirmed. A browser-visible eAIP is not an app data licence.

### India

AAI clearly publishes the authoritative eAIP and identifies the national AIS/NOF structure, but Phase 0 research did not find machine-feed specifications or commercial redistribution terms. The product should approach AAI for a data-provider agreement and DGCA for regulatory scoping rather than scrape the site.

### Global fallback

OurAirports and OSM are valuable for discovery, simulation, search seeding, and visual context. Their openness does not supply operational authority or completeness. openAIP's non-commercial licence is not a safe default for a commercial product without written clarification. No global fallback should allow the UI to imply current authoritative navigation coverage.

## 4. Procurement questions for every provider

1. Does the licence permit commercial mobile use, backend transformation, offline caching, user downloads, derived vector/raster products, and redistribution to end users?
2. Are charts, metadata, raw data, derived data, names/logos, and third-party components governed separately?
3. What geography, fields, accuracy/integrity, completeness, history, preview cycle, and urgent-correction feed are included?
4. What are publication/latency SLA, maintenance windows, rate limits, authentication, support, and incident notice obligations?
5. Can data be retained after termination, and how must expiry, revocation, correction, and user-device deletion work?
6. What attribution, click-through terms, audit, usage reporting, export-control, and user-count royalties apply?
7. Does the provider authorize use for in-flight situational awareness, route planning, weather briefing, alerts, or only general information?
8. May independently sourced datasets be combined, and what share-alike/confidentiality restrictions apply to the result?
9. What quality assurance evidence, checksums/CRCs, and liability/warranty terms are provided?
10. What exact wording may describe source authority and official-briefing/approval status?

## 5. Immediate next action

Run a two-week U.S. ingestion proof using current and preview FAA NASR, FAA DOF, AWC API samples, USGS/Copernicus terrain samples, and WMM test vectors **without redistributing artifacts**. In parallel, obtain written rights answers from FAA AIS, AAI AIS, NATS AIS, and EUROCONTROL. The proof should measure schema drift, source conflicts, package size, cycle activation, urgent correction handling, and offline validation before any mobile dataset commitment.
